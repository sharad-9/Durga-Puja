The Essence of Durga Puja

Durga Puja is more than a festival; it's a grand celebration of culture, heritage, and the victory of good over evil. For five days, the air is filled with the rhythmic beats of the 'dhaak', the fragrant smoke of incense, and boundless joy.

The Five Days of Celebration

Shashthi

The festival begins. The divine face of the Goddess Durga is unveiled, and rituals like 'Bodhon' and 'Amontron' officially welcome her.

Saptami

The 'Pran Pratisthan' ritual is performed, infusing the idol with the divine presence. The 'Nabapatrika' or 'Kola Bou' is given a ceremonial bath.

Ashtami

The pinnacle of the puja. The 'Kumari Puja' sees a young girl worshipped as a form of the Goddess, and the powerful 'Sandhi Puja' is performed at dusk.

Nabami

The final day of worship, concluding with a grand 'Maha Arati'. It is a day of joyous celebration before the farewell.

Dashami

The day of immersion. Married women perform 'Sindur Khela', smearing vermilion on each other. The idol is then immersed in water, symbolizing her return home.
